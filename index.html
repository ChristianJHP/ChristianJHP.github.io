    The Magnetic Accelerator

In this setup, we've used a photoresistor alongside a MOSFET to create a fascinating and dynamic experience. The photoresistor, which is sensitive to light, plays a central role in this setup. It's connected to the MOSFET's gate terminal, and together with a resistor, they create a simple voltage control system. When the ferromagnetic ball comes into contact with the light falling on the photoresistor, it essentially blocks the light, causing the photoresistor's resistance to increase instantly. This triggers a decrease in voltage at the MOSFET's gate, prompting it to switch on. As the MOSFET activates, it completes a circuit that powers an electromagnet, enabling the flow of electricity. The electromagnet, now energized, generates a magnetic field that attracts the ferromagnetic ball. As the ball gets closer to the electromagnet, it gains speed and kinetic energy.

What's particularly fascinating about this setup is its self-sustaining nature: once initiated, the ferromagnetic ball continues to pass over the photoresistor, ensuring the MOSFET remains in the "on" state, and the electromagnet consistently propels the ball forward. This creates an indefinitely mesmerizing display of motion and magnetism, illustrating how basic electronic components can come together to create an engaging and endlessly captivating experiment.
